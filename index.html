Stage 2 · Aligned Question: Does my message meet their real life?

Step 0 · Bring In Your Core Message

Paste the short version you wrote in the Client-Lens Magnet tool. We will reuse this line in different ways, not reinvent it each time.

Example: I help work-from-home moms who also homeschool create calm, repeatable weekly plans so their work and lessons both get the time they need.

Step 1 · Map Their Real Day

You’re naming what their life actually looks like, what isn’t working, how that feels, and what needs to change for them to say “yes”.

Example: juggling client work, homeschool blocks, and house tasks in the same space and hours.

Example: plans fall apart by Tuesday, constant switching between roles, never feeling on top of it.

Example: tension, guilt, racing thoughts at night, snapping at kids or clients, feeling like they’re failing at all roles.

Example: calmer mornings, a simple weekly plan, time blocks that protect both income and kids, and more breathing room.

Connector
Your message works best when it says: “I see the kind of day you’re living, I get what keeps breaking, I know how that actually feels, and I’m clear about what gets better and why it’s worth it.”

Step 2 · Choose One Channel and Intent

We’ll shape one piece of messaging at a time, on purpose instead of posting just to post.

Why this matters
Without choosing an intent, it’s easy to write a post or email that feels busy but doesn’t actually move anyone. This step helps you say one clear thing, to one person, for one reason.

Step 3 · Build a Simple Flow

We’ll turn what you wrote into a tiny story: meet them where they are, name what hurts, point to what can change, and offer a next step.

One sentence that makes them think “that’s my day”.

2–3 lines that link what’s hard now to what could be different with your support.

Stage 2 · Aligned (Pre‑GTM)

This lab assumes you have a core line from the Client-Lens Magnet. It helps you match that line to your buyer’s real day, and shape it into messages that are kind to both of you.

How this connects

System Building Playground → Client-Lens Magnet → Messaging Alignment Lab. From internal system, to one core client line, to small pieces of copy you can reuse in email, posts, pages, and conversations.

How to use this if you’re busy & neurospicy

  • Do this in short passes: Step 0–1 today, the rest later.
  • Use bullet phrases. You can smooth into full sentences at the end.
  • Repeat with the same persona but different intent: recognize vs. decide vs. act.
  • Notice which channel feels easiest to write for; you can lean on that first.

How this ties into your system

System Building Playground gave you the big picture of your offers and flow. The Client-Lens Magnet gave you one core buyer-facing line. This lab ties that line to real people, real days, and real channels so when you go to Steward tools, you’re not guessing — you’re repeating.

Shared Language · Aligned

Core line
Your short, client-facing sentence about who you help and what you help them do.
Buyer reality
The actual day, problems, feelings, and payoffs in your person’s life — not just your offer features.
Snippet
A small piece of copy (one hook, one story, one invite) that carries the same core message.
Intent
The specific job a message is doing: helping them see themselves, decide fit, gauge timing, or take a next step.
Readiness note: If you feel totally stuck naming a specific person, day, and payoff, you may still be in a “listening and validation” season. That’s okay. You can use this lab as a sandbox and return after a few more real conversations or Rooted work.
